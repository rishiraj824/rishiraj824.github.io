We often forget grounding techniques or are just not self-aware to realize what grounds us on a day-to-day basis. Having been with myself and just myself, I've learned a lot over these past two months.

Your body signals you what your brain needs to do and you keep ignoring it, but eventually when it decides to go into fight or flight mode you realize there's so much more overstimulation going on which you keep feeding your brain - stressors from the outside world, junk from social media, endless doomscrolling or negative propaganda or things which create self-doubt. It could also be just your own thoughts which are self-deprecating and not helping you heal.

## The Journey of Healing

When healing is in progress you are constantly reading about your brain, about how your thoughts work in harmony with your brain and your subconscious mind listens to your conscious brain. The book _The Power of Your Subconscious Mind_ changed so much in me. Negative self-talk and anxiety of things just ruins our body's response to anything and think rationally.

You are supposed to be mentally and emotionally regulated. Sometimes those stressors can cause serious problems and what you realize in those moments is that grounding techniques and constantly enforcing positive thoughts are the key. Your conscious mind is the feeder to your subconscious mind - it listens to what you are consuming, reading, and what your intuition is responsible for is all coming from your conscious brain.

## Feeding the Mind Right

We need to feed ourselves the right food, the right people, have deeper connections and have the right set of tools with us for mindfulness to keep us going. These can come handy to anyone in pain. Self-aware people and people who are forward-moving are always keeping those in their pocket.

## Understanding Fight or Flight

Your fight or flight response was something your body has learned and experienced in danger to get food, shelter and water in the past. Those instincts can't just go away and are there to protect you. When your body is going through those emotions it means it's releasing those hormones (adrenaline) to keep you safe.

Remember that the innate quality of your mind and body is to protect yourself from harm and it will do that regardless of what happens. It's self-healing and it wants homeostasis (the balance of dopamine and pain) and it will try to be in that state for the longest time. The parasympathetic nervous system will take over and your body will always want to come back to the calm state.

## The Power of Awareness

Just by knowing these facts you can almost always say that you are safe and protected. Of course, if you are in a fire or in an earthquake you can't fake the reality, but sometimes when the brain is functioning out of your own preconjectured negative thoughts it automatically creates those fake scenarios and triggers an unwanted fight or flight response. I didn't know this was the case and people had to know this. A great learning for me.
